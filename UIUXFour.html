REDCOM SIGMA UI Re-design

Not much cannot be said about this project due to a NDA signed

Description

 

My team did a usability testing on a company's application to find out usability issues in the application. After the heuristic evaluation we conducted a study with participants to find the problems and get to solutions.

 

Overview

 
 

  The Sigma Client Windows Application allows communication between n-number of users via via chat, voice and video calls. This application uses two types of accounts, XMPP for chat and SIP for voice/video. In order to gain more knowledge on how to improve the user’s experience while communicating using Sigma Client application, the REDCOM team wanted usability students at Rochester Institute of Technology to conduct Heuristic Evaluation and run Usability Test on their application. Our primary goal is to identify areas in which users may encounter problems while completing basic tasks on the Sigma Client application. This report includes our findings and proposes some improvements to the interface of the application that will further encourage users to prefer using this applications without having any difficulty. The two major usability problem in this application that were discovered by our team fall under following heuristic: Group like widgets/function and External Consistency. Our team has recommended certain alterations to fix the design problems in order to clarify the use of the widgets and provide user with better experience.

Heuristic Evaluation

 
 

  List of heuristic evaluations used:

1. External Consistency
2. Widgets and labels near widgets
3. Group like widgets/functions
4. Frequently used functions optimized
5. Speak the User’s Language
6. Perceptibility of Feedback
7. Perceptibility of System State
8. Internal Consistency
9. Appropriate Selection of Design Patterns
10. Minimize knowledge in the Head
11. User Control and Freedom
12. Error Prevention
13. Error Recovery
14. Novel interactions easily learned and recalled
15. Help & Documentation

1.External Consistency

Definition: The system uses interactions and design patterns that are consistent with the platform and analogous systems.

General Impression: Most interactions are externally consistent. The application does not have a “Call” functionality in the dial window.
Issue Description: The phone application for dialing numbers in smartphones and in other VOIP applications usually have a call start and call end button. This feature is not there in the Sigma application.
Possible Cause: The users have to change their behavior and will be forced to search for the call function.
Suggestion Fix: A call start and call end button feature can embedded in the application.

2. Widgets and labels near widgets

Definition: Place widgets (controls) adjacent to their respective targets of operation and labels on, or directly adjacent to, their associated controls. General Impression: Items that the drop down from the menu bar should be labelled correctly. The redundant arrows for expansion can confuse the end user and thus should be eliminated from the list. The label and icons should be externally consistent.

Issue Description:
2.1. The call history and contact list icons are overlapping and are placed in the peripheral vision. The functions may not be mapped correctly and will not be perceived by the user.
2.2. Icons are repeated for Account Information and Contact Information under Tools dropdown.
2.3. Call forwarding does not have a dropdown as suggested by the forward triangle icon. Call start and call end button can be added Overlapping - call history & contact list icons No drop-down as suggested by the arrow and Repeated icons contact list call history.
2.4. The blue bar under the Audio tab in Options menu does not have applicable labels to the widgets.
Problem cause: Many labels are not named correctly or do not have any indication at all.
Suggestion Fix: Labels should be named with their appropriate labels adjacent to the widgets

Research Questions

 
 

  We plan on collecting Quantitative Data like:

1. How much time does it take for the participants to make a call to another user?
2. Are participants able to adjust the audio settings in the application to improve their sound quality for voice communication?
3. How easily can the participants change the wrong number or a wrongly spelled contact after typing it in the search bar?
4. What obstacles do the participants face when trying to save a contact to a speed dial button?
5. How difficult is it to find and create a conference call?
6. Can participants find a way to hide the speed dial buttons on the screen?
7. How long does it take for the participants to find their call history?
8. Can participants test if their audio out and notification sounds are working?
9. Are participants able to check the input level of the microphone on the screen?
10. Can participants use the call-forwarding feature in the application?
11. How long and how much iteration does users take to get a clear video without lag during a video call?
12. How many users in the call (apart from the user who has added the new user) are able to figure out immediately if another user has joined the conference call?
13. How long does it take for users to realize if they have selected correct option (XMPP/SIP) while adding a contact to the list?

We also plan on collecting Qualitative Data like:

14. Participants will think aloud along the task and will need to explain if they get confused or stuck on a certain task.
15. Are participants comfortable not having a save button in the options menu after they have changed a few settings? How many times do participants feel the need for a save button in the option menu?
16. Do users feel that the call history log is too technical and difficult to understand? In one-to-one communication, can a user identify if another user has joined the call or left it? (join and left actions have similar tones)?
17. Do users want shortcut keys? If so, what functions do they want the shortcut keys for?
18. Will participants be able to understand the buttons on the screen during a call?
19. What do the users think about the hold tone?
20. There will be a post test questionnaire conducted, to understand how the participants felt about using the application and to get additional information about the application.
21. Do participants require any additional features or options?
22. What does the participant think about the user interface and design of the application?
23. What other applications do users find this application similar to? And how well does it compare with these other applications?
